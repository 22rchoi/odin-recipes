Sheikh el Mehshi

Don't be mesheh

Baby zucchini filled with ground meat, onions, and pine nuts, cooked in a silky, garlicky, and minty yogurt sauce.

At the end, you'll have a delicious meal served with a side of warm rice.

Ingredients

The Zucchini

  • 2 kg baby zucchini(kousa)

  • 1 recipe of Hashweh

  • 1 L vegetable/other neutral oil with a high smoke point

Yogurt sauce

  • 1.5 kg plain full-fat yogurt

  • 1 head garlic, smashed or minced finely

  • 1 tbsp dried mint, 2 tbsp labneh(strained yogurt), 4 tbsp corn starch 3 tbsp vegetable oil, 2 tsp salt

Steps

  1. Core the zucchini, taking out as much of the insides as possible, while being careful to not puncture a hole through the sides

  2. Scoop in the Hashweh into the emptied zucchini with a spoon and push it down with your thumb, until its as full as possible

  3. Add oil to a stockpot a couple inches high and heat to 350F/180C. Fry the stuffed zucchini in batches, turning occasionally, until it's golden on all side(it'll take around 5 minutes). Drain on a paper towel-lined baking sheet. Zucchini can be reserved in the fridge or freezer at this point if dividing the cooking tasks across multiple days

  4. On the day of serving, bring about 1 L of water to a boil with the garlic, mint, and salt. Add the fried zucchini and simmer until fully cooked, taking around 3-5 minutes. If your fingernail easily pierces the flesh, then it's ready. Strain, reserve the liquid.

  5. Get a stainless steel stockpot. Combine 2 tbsp of water with the corn starch in a small bowl. Add the yogurt, labneh, and corn starch/water mixture to the pot over high heat. Using a whisk, mix constantly and let it boil for about a minute to activate the corn starch.

  6. Add the zucchini cooking liquid to the yogurt mixture steadily until it reaches desired consistency, almost like a thick soup. It will also thicken up a bit with additional cooking.

  7. Bring it back up to a boil and add the zucchini. Cook for about 2-3 minutes or until heated through. Adjust seasoning(if needed), and serve with rice.

And with that, you're done! If you want to store leftovers, cool everything completely before covering and refrigerating. To reheat, bring to a gentle simmer over the stove, thinning the sauce with water if needed.
