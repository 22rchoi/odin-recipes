Ghormeh Sabzi

Be a Gourmet(sabzi)

This khormesh, or stew, is often called Iran's national dish

The different herbs - fenugreek leaves, omari limes, and more - all mix to form a delicious and complex flavor.

Ingredients

  • 1 1/2 pounds lamb shoulder or beef chuck - trimmed and cut into 2-inch pieces

  • 1 teaspoon - ground turmeric, sea salt, and freshly ground black pepper

  • 3 tablespoons plus 1/4 cup extra-virgin olive oil

  • Polo Ba Tahdig (Persian Rice With Bread Crust)

  • Mast-o Khiar or plain yogurt

Now, time for the herbs

  • 1/2 cup dried kidney beans, 1 tablespoon dried fenugreek leaves, 1/4 teaspoon crumbled saffron threads

  • 1 pound Italian parsley, 1 pound cilantro (about 3 large bunches each)

  • 1 large yellow onion, thinly sliced, 2 bunches chives, 1 bunch scallions, roots trimmed, and 4 Omari(dried Persian) limes, punctured multiple times with a fork

Steps

  1. In a medium bowl, season the meat with turmeric, 2 teaspoons salt and 1/2 teaspoon pepper. Set aside for later

  2. Rinse the beans and place in another medium bowl with 1 cup water and a generous pinch of salt. Set aside to soak for 30 minutes

  3. Meanwhile, place a large Dutch oven/pot over medium-high heat, and add 3 tablespoons oil. When it shimmers, add meat and cook, turning regularly so that it browns evenly, for about 15 minutes. Once the meat has browned, move it to the edges of the pot and add the onion along with some salt to the center of the pot. Caramelize the onion, 8 to 10 minutes.

  4. Drain the beans and add to the pot, stir to combine everything. Add 4 cups of water, increase heat to a boil. Then, reduce heat to medium-low, and let it simmer for 2 hours

  5. While waiting, prepare the herbs - wash them, remove the stems - and chop the leaves/stems very, very finely. The more finely chopped the stronger the ghormeh sabzi.

  6. Separately chop the chives and scallions(including the green tops) as finely as possible by hand

  7. Set a large frying pan over medium heat and once it's hot, add the remaining 1/4 cup oil and scallion-chive mixture. Allow it to wilt, then add all remaining chopped herbs, crushing fenugreek leaves between your fingers as you add them. Cook, stirring continuously, until the herbs are wilted and very dark green, and give off a bright green oil when pressed with a spoon. This step'll take 18-20 minutes, and is crucial to the flavor and color of the stew.

  8. When the meat has cooked for 2 hours, add the cooked herb mixture, Omari limes, and 1/2 cup water. Season with salt and bring to a boil, then reduce, and let simmer for 1 hour. Check on limes to make sure they're submerged in the stew but not falling apart. If still floating after 20 minutes, gently push them into the stew

  9. As the stew nears the 3-hour mark, remove the lid and check the meat; it should be tender, but if the stew seems watery, leave it uncovered and reduce. Taste and adjust with salt and pepper, and if acidity is needed, juice some lime into it until sufficiently tangy. Stir in the saffron, and at the end, the stew should be a very deep, dark shade of green and quite thick. Return dried limes into the stew to serve.

  10. Serve hot with the Persian rice and mast-o khiar

Finally, enjoy!
