Chicken Pho

Pho'cking delicious mate!

Pho is, without a doubt, the most popular food associated with Vietnam.

In this recipe, I'll be focusing on Beef Pho's easier-to-make(but just as delicious!), lesser-known cousin, Chicken Pho!

Ingredients

The Broth

  • Chicken - use thighs, or even a whole chicken! Don't substitute the meat with chicken breast, since the broth needs dark meat and skin for maximum flavor

  • Spices - cloves, fennel, star anise, coriander, and black cardamom. Give the broth that beloved, distinct Pho fragrance

  • Onions and ginger - traditional aromatics to add a hint of smokiness to the broth

  • Fish sauce - this'll make the broth more complex, and give it a deeper flavor

  • Sugar - Pho broth is actually a bit sweet, and even though this usually goes unnoticed, if you don't use sugar, the broth'll seem lacking

The Toppings

Not all of these are needed, but try to get at least 2 or 3 for that fresh herb quality characteristic of Vietnamese food.

Lime, coriander/cilantro, green onion, thai basil, mint, beansprouts, noodles, or chili(if you're feeling spicy)

Steps

  1. Char onion and ginger and don't be afraid, burn these well to provide a subtle smokey fragrance signature to Pho broth!

  2. Simmer everything GENTLY for 1.5 hrs to infuse the water with all those incredible flavourings

  3. Strain, shred chicken, serve with Toppings!

And that's it! Now, the only thing to do is to pho'ly enjoy your meal.
